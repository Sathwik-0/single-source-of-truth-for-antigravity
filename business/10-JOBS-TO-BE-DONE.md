# Jobs To Be Done (JTBD)

Chronicle is hired by engineering organizations to fulfill specific, high-value functional and emotional progress. Below are the core jobs users hire Chronicle to perform.

---

## Job 1: Understand History
* **User Statement:** *"When I inherit a system or touch legacy code, help me understand its history so I can modify it safely."*
* **Functional Dimension:** Retrieve the code evolution, associated PR discussions, developer comments, and specifications.
* **Emotional Dimension:** Confidence that making a change will not break hidden dependencies or violate undocumented design constraints.
* **Success Criteria:** Zero guesswork. The engineer can modify legacy modules with full context of prior decisions.

---

## Job 2: Investigate Incidents
* **User Statement:** *"When production breaks under my watch, help me explain why it happened and what changed so I can resolve it immediately."*
* **Functional Dimension:** Build a causal timeline of recent code deployments, configuration updates, database migrations, and Slack communications.
* **Emotional Dimension:** Calmness and control during high-pressure outage scenarios.
* **Success Criteria:** Reduction in Mean Time to Resolution (MTTR) from hours to minutes by isolating the exact change and discussion that triggered the incident.

---

## Job 3: Recover Decisions
* **User Statement:** *"Help me reconstruct why this architecture was chosen months ago so we don't repeat old debates or reverse correct decisions."*
* **Functional Dimension:** Aggregate the RFC docs, email threads, Slack channel reviews, and code prototypes that formed the final architecture approval.
* **Emotional Dimension:** Relief that historical context is preserved and team alignment is maintained.
* **Success Criteria:** Accessing a chronological decision audit log that outlines the trade-offs, benchmarks, and participants.

---

## Job 4: Onboard Faster
* **User Statement:** *"Help me become a productive contributor to this team quickly without depending entirely on senior developers' time."*
* **Functional Dimension:** Map out codebase architecture, domain ownership, service boundaries, and core workflows automatically.
* **Emotional Dimension:** Autonomy, belonging, and a sense of progress as a new hire.
* **Success Criteria:** Writing and deploying production code with high confidence in the first week.

---

## Job 5: Preserve Knowledge
* **User Statement:** *"Prevent our hard-earned engineering context and system knowledge from walking out the door when developers leave."*
* **Functional Dimension:** Continuous, background extraction and indexing of discussions, code review comments, and post-mortems.
* **Emotional Dimension:** Security and peace of mind that the company's knowledge base is permanent and robust.
* **Success Criteria:** The team's collective intelligence remains queryable and highly accurate regardless of personnel changes.

# Go-To-Market (GTM) Strategy

Chronicle's distribution strategy relies on bottom-up developer adoption, virality through shared evidence links, and land-and-expand conversion metrics.

---

## 1. Initial Target Audience
We focus our initial GTM resources on:
* **Senior Engineers:** Tech leads who suffer the most from context-switching and tribal knowledge demands.
* **Early-Stage Tech Startups (5-50 engineers):** Fast-growing teams where documentation is neglected, and codebase complexity is growing exponentially.
* **Tech Founders & CTOs:** Leaders who want to protect the codebase context and scale their team without experiencing severe knowledge drop-off.

---

## 2. Distribution Channels
* **GitHub Marketplace:** Direct integration with repository installation flows.
* **Slack App Directory:** A quick installation to index incident channels and workspace communication.
* **Developer Communities:** Active engagement in Discord/Slack communities for startups and developers.
* **Open Source Utility Tools:** Offering lightweight, local-only code analysis modules to developers for free, driving awareness to our cloud memory engine.

---

## 3. The Activation Loop
To convert a lead into an active team user:

```text
  1. Connection: Developer connects a GitHub repo or Slack channel (Takes 2 minutes).
  2. First Query: Developer asks a historical question (e.g., "Why is this caching logic here?").
  3. The "Wow" Moment: Chronicle delivers a fast, citation-accurate answer with side-by-side evidence panels.
  4. Trust Increase: Developer confirms the citation and gets the context.
  5. Peer Expansion: Developer shares the answer link in a Slack channel to help a teammate, driving team-wide awareness.
```

---

## 4. Land-and-Expand Growth
* **Land:** An individual developer or tech lead adopts the Pro plan to index their primary repositories.
* **Team Adoption:** The developer shares Chronicle citations during sprint planning or incident reviews, leading the team to connect their Slack channels and adopt the Team plan.
* **Enterprise Upgrade:** As more teams adopt Chronicle, the CTO/VPE upgrades the entire engineering organization to the Enterprise plan for SSO, RBAC, and SOC2 compliance.
